About Us

COEP Sunrisers is a student group at College of Engineering, Pune where the members work together to design, fabricate, test and race a solar-electric vehicle. The group consists of students from various interdisciplinary streams, working in concurrence to build a light weight, emission-free prototype of an environmento-friendly means of transport. The team was founded in August, 2015 by PG students of COEP from the Automotive division. The team completed its first vehicle "Aethon" in February 2016. The vehicle took part in Electric Solar Vehicle Championship 2016, Bhopal.

The 4 disciplines we work upon are:

MECHANICAL

Using the principles of vehicle dynamics, chasis, suspension, steering, brakes, are focused upon ensusring minimum weight of the vehicle and maximum st ability of the passengers.


ELECTRONICS

Battery, battery level indicator, sensors for temperature, speed and proximity are few of the electronic components that are custom-made by the team.


TRANSMISSION

With the inclusion of CVT, differential, BL DC motor as the main parts of the vehicle transmission, the vehicle is a magnificent instance of where mechanical and electrical subsystems go hand-in-hand



SOLAR PANEL

The extremely light-weight and flexible solar panels have to be mounted in such a way that aerodynamics and in such a way that aerodynamics and aesthetics of the vehicle are preserved.

Achievements
  • "Aethon" successfully cleared the technical inspection, safety test, brake test, in its first attempt in EVSC.
  • In the dynamic round, the vehicle secured 2nd place in Endurance and an Overall 3rd position.
COMPETITIONS

ISIE-Electric solar vehicle championship is Asia's largest solar championship. The scope of the competition is to simulate research into, and development of, sustainable transport. The inception of this competition took place in 2013, at Punjab University. The number of participating teams in this tournament are increasing ever since. In 2016, 92 teams participated in this nationwide competition.
